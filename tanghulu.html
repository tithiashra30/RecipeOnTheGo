Tanghulu

Preparation Time: 20 minutes

Tanghulu is a traditional Chinese candied fruit snack, often made with hawthorn berries or other fruits dipped in hardened sugar syrup.

Ingredients

Procedure

  • Rinse and dry the fruits completely. Remove any stems if necessary.
  • Thread the fruits onto bamboo skewers (3-4 per skewer).
  • In a saucepan, combine sugar and water. Heat on medium without stirring.
  • Boil until the syrup reaches 300°F (hard crack stage). Test by dropping into cold water—it should harden immediately.
  • Dip each skewer into the hot sugar syrup, coating evenly.
  • Place on a greased surface or parchment paper to cool and harden.
  • Optionally, sprinkle with sesame seeds for added flavor.
  • Enjoy your crispy, sweet Tanghulu!
